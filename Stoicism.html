Stoicism

Have you heard about stoicism?


Zeno of Citium (334-262 BC)

Founder of Stoicism. He began teaching in the Stoa Poikile (Painted Porch) in Athens.

Cleanthes (331-232 BC)

Successor of Zeno. Known for his work in ethics and his hymn to Zeus.

Chrysippus (279-206 BC)

Third head of the Stoic school. Significant contributions to logic, ethics, and physics.

Epictetus (50-135 AD)

Former slave who became a prominent Stoic philosopher. His teachings were recorded by his student Arrian.

Marcus Aurelius (121-180 AD)

Roman Emperor and Stoic philosopher. Known for his work "Meditations".
